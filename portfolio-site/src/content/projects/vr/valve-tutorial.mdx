## Overview

The VR Valve Tutorial is a practical training module designed to help apprentices and technicians learn industrial valve assembly through direct, hands-on interaction. Built for Meta Quest, it allows users to practise assembly steps in a controlled, risk-free environment before working with physical equipment.

The experience focuses on clarity and repetition. Users are guided through each stage of the assembly process with contextual prompts and visual callouts that stay anchored to the relevant components. This allows learners to understand both *what* they are doing and *why*, without breaking flow.

Rather than replacing hands-on training, the module is designed to support it. The goal is to shorten ramp-up time, reduce early mistakes, and give learners confidence before they step onto the workshop floor.

## Key Features:

- Hands-on VR assembly workflow  
  Users assemble valve components step by step using natural interactions, reinforcing correct procedures through practice rather than observation.
- Contextual callout system  
  An interactive callout UI links instructions directly to physical parts, keeping guidance visible without pulling users out of the task.
- Real-time feedback and validation  
  The system checks each step as it happens, helping prevent incorrect assembly and reinforcing correct technique.
- Designed for repeatable training  
  Sessions can be replayed as often as needed, making it suitable for onboarding, refreshers, and skills reinforcement.
- Built for standalone VR  
  Optimised for Meta Quest with a focus on performance, comfort, and ease of deployment in training environments.
- Refined through user testing  
  Iterated based on feedback from real users to improve clarity, pacing, and usability.

## Technology Stack:
- Engine: Unity
- Programming Language: C#
- XR Framework: Oculus SDK / OpenXR
- Target Platforms: Meta Quest

## Project Goal & Learning Outcomes:
- Support industrial training with VR  
  Provide a structured way for learners to practise complex assembly tasks before handling physical equipment.
- Reduce onboarding friction  
  Help new starters build familiarity and confidence earlier in the training process.
- Improve consistency in training  
  Ensure the same steps, guidance, and checks are applied every time, regardless of instructor availability.
- Explore practical XR in industry  
  Demonstrate how VR can be used as a supporting tool in real-world vocational and technical education.
